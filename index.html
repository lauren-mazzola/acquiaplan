Lauren MazzolaPipegen Strategy
Pipeline campaign plays
3 big plays across Source, DAM and Cloud
Rather than a new topic each week, the team runs three product plays simultaneously across the book. Each play has a clear product focus, a specific value message and a set of target personas. The goal is to give reps a relevant, confident reason to reach out to the right person at every account, running all three motions at once rather than sequencing them one at a time.
Product Play name Target persona Core message Outreach angle
Source Kill the dev bottleneck. Marketing builds what it needs, when it needs it. VP of Digital Marketing · Demand Gen Manager · Marketing Ops Marketing teams are losing weeks to dev queues for landing pages, microsites and campaign pages. Source gives marketers the ability to build and launch without engineering dependency. Lead with a win story from a comparable customer. Ask about their current time to launch a campaign page and what that delay is costing them in lost pipeline. For existing DAM customers, frame this as closing the loop: your assets are already in DAM, now put them on a page your team can actually build.
DAM One source of truth for every asset your brand produces. Brand Manager · Creative Ops Lead · Content Director · Marketing Director Brand and content teams waste hours hunting for the right asset version across shared drives, email threads and agency portals. DAM's AI-powered search, focal point cropping and permissions model solves this at scale. Lead with the productivity angle. Time spent finding assets instead of using them is a metric every brand team feels. For accounts working with external agencies, lead with brand protection and control. For content-heavy teams, lead with AI-powered video creation and the ability to manage the full content lifecycle from one platform.
Cloud Enterprise Drupal infrastructure that scales without slowing your team down. VP of Engineering · Director of Web Platform · CISO · DevOps Lead As digital experience demands grow, engineering teams feel the pressure of scaling, uptime, security and developer velocity. Cloud Platform removes infrastructure burden, gives devs modern tooling and meets enterprise security and compliance requirements including FedRAMP. For engineering leaders, lead with developer velocity and the ability to ship faster with less risk using Cloud IDE, Code Studio and Pipelines. For IT and security leaders, lead with FedRAMP authorization and enterprise-grade compliance. For organizations managing multiple Drupal properties, lead with multi-site management and the cost of running fragmented infrastructure.
How to use these plays: Each play gives reps a product-specific reason to reach out that feels relevant and timely. Pair the outreach angle with a customer win story where available, personalize using account research and tie the conversation to something happening in the customer's business. The goal is a thought-leadership touchpoint that opens a real conversation, not a product pitch. Reps should run all three plays simultaneously across their book rather than focusing on one product at a time.

AI and tools workflow
My AI and tools playbook for manager and team
I use AI and internal tools every day as infrastructure, not as a crutch. From morning prioritization to EBR building to win story documentation, the right tools let me and my team operate at a level of preparation and personalization that creates a real edge. Here's exactly how I use them and how I'll roll them out across the team.
My rollout approach Model it first, then make it a team habit
I'll start using AI and internal tools visibly in my own workflow from day one and let the team see the value in action before I introduce them formally. By week five I'll run a show-and-tell: here's how I use these tools, here's what they save me and here's one thing I want each of you to try this week. By day 90 every rep has 3–4 consistent habits.
How I use AI and tools in my own workflow in days 1–30
Morning email and calendar prioritization
Every morning I use AI to get a prioritized summary of what needs a response today, what's time-sensitive and what can wait. During a transition period when everything feels urgent this keeps me focused on what actually matters most.
1:1 and leadership meeting prep
Before every rep 1:1 or leadership meeting I use notes and internal tools to build a structured prep brief showing where things left off, what to follow up on and one observation to lead with.
Forecast narrative prep
Before every forecast call I structure my narrative including the number, confidence level, key deals driving it, risk deals to watch and what I need from leadership. A clean, well-structured forecast story builds credibility fast.
Internal risk documents
For any at-risk account or deal I draft a structured risk brief with account background, identified risks and mitigation plan. I present these proactively to D'Arcy before they surface in a QBR.
Coaching plan drafting
After synthesizing 1:1 notes and call observations I use AI to help organize a structured coaching plan with strengths, development areas, specific actions and timeline across 8–9 reps.
EBR and QBR building
Internal tools help assemble the narrative structure starting with business outcomes achieved, moving to value delivered and then pivoting to future roadmap and expansion.
Rolling these tools out to the team starting week 5
Pre-call company research briefs
Before any discovery call or onsite: company news, financial health, known tech stack, industry trends and likely business priorities. The single biggest lever for consultative selling.
Compete play prep
Pull up the known competitor and use case and use internal tools to get competitor strengths, likely objections and the Acquia differentiation narrative.
Prospecting message drafting
Using research tools to understand a stakeholder's role and priorities, reps build a personalized first-touch message that leads with something relevant rather than a product pitch.
Win story documentation
After a significant customer win, AI helps draft a structured win story from call notes and CRM activity. Turns a conversation into a reusable prospecting asset in minutes.
Email drafting and follow-ups
AI drafts follow-up emails after customer conversations. The rep makes it their own. Cuts drafting time and improves quality especially for newer reps.
Onsite and conference logistics pages
For every scheduled onsite: one URL with agenda, attendee bios, location details, key talking points and account context. Customers notice the prep.

Strategic account planning
My big bet account framework
This is a structured, living account plan framework I'll introduce to the team for high-priority accounts where the stakes are high enough to warrant a cross-functional, milestone-driven approach. It's not a document that gets filled out and forgotten. It's a working tool that keeps everyone aligned, surfaces gaps before they kill a deal and ensures nothing falls through the cracks between now and close.
What this framework is and why it matters
The big bet account plan is designed to make reps go through every critical activity required to drive a large, complex deal across the finish line. It forces cross-functional thinking by involving the right Acquia stakeholders, mapping the right customer contacts and ensuring competitive, technical, financial and relationship gaps are all actively addressed. Any deal over $15K gets this treatment. Internal tools and AI help reps research and populate the plan but the rep owns every relationship and conversation behind it.

My motivation
Why I want this role
The plan matters. The preparation matters. But none of it means anything without the right reasons behind it. Here's mine, honest, personal and in my own words.
The honest answer
I want to lead
because I'm built for it.
Not because it's the next title on a ladder. Because I genuinely love helping people get better, I think in systems and strategies, and I've spent years watching teams win and lose and developing the skills and strategies to continue to improve and understand what made the difference. This is the right moment, the right team and the right company to do this work.
📈
I want to be on a leadership track and I'm ready to invest in it seriously
I've thought carefully about where I want my career to go and leadership is the clear answer. Not as an abstract goal but as something I want to build deliberately, starting here, with this team, learning from people I already respect and trust.
🤝
I learn best from leaders I already have real relationships with
I know D'Arcy. I know how he leads. I've seen Vicki operate at a high level and I've carried that with me. There's something rare about stepping into a role where you trust the people above you and believe they'll invest in you the way you'll invest in your team.
🌱
Helping other people grow is where I do my best work
I've always been the person other reps came to with questions, deal strategy or when they needed to think something through. That was never a burden. It was energizing. I genuinely want to see the people on my team succeed and I know that when they do, the numbers follow.
💡
I'm naturally creative and this role lets me build things
I think differently about problems. I'm drawn to building systems, testing new approaches and finding creative ways to generate pipeline or develop people. A manager role lets me build plays, design the workflow and create something that outlasts any single quarter.
🔥
I'm coming back because I believe in what Acquia is building
I left. I learned things somewhere else. And I'm choosing to come back because the product direction is more compelling than when I left, the leadership team is one I want to work with and the momentum coming out of Engage feels like something I can genuinely help accelerate.
🐾
And most importantly, I'm doing this for Dooley
Dooley is my one-year-old golden retriever. He's awesome and deserves a backyard! This role is part of getting there. If that's not a reason to bring your best every single day, I don't know what is.
